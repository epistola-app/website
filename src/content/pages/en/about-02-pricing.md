title: "Budget-driven pricing, not profit maximization"
---
We set clear goals each year: what features we want to build, what infrastructure we need, what SLAs we commit to, and what team we can afford. We budget for those goals. Then we price the software to cover that budget—nothing more, nothing less.

Our revenue comes from three streams: feature development, SLA contracts, and donations. Not every organization needs or wants an SLA, but we still maintain the software, ship security patches, and keep things running. These donations—we call them levensvatbaarheidsbijdragen (viability contributions)—help keep the project sustainable for everyone.

If revenue grows beyond our budget, we have two choices: reinvest the surplus into the product and team, or reduce prices. Returns on investment are capped—we don't accumulate profit to maximize shareholder returns.
